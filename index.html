Conditions we support

You may not have been
given the right options yet

ANAVA specialises in conditions where conventional approaches have not provided adequate relief. Select a condition to learn more.

Treatment-Resistant Depression

When two or more antidepressants have not provided relief, neurotherapy may open a pathway forward.

TMS and tDCS have demonstrated meaningful efficacy in patients with treatment-resistant depression, working directly at the neural circuits involved in mood regulation — without systemic side effects. Clinical outcomes show significant improvement in a majority of patients who have not responded to pharmacotherapy alone.

Learn more

Chronic Pain Management

Persistent pain that standard treatments haven't relieved may respond to targeted neuromodulation.

Chronic pain often involves central sensitisation — the brain's pain-processing networks become dysregulated over time. ANAVA's neurotherapy protocols target these networks directly, modulating pain perception at its neurological source rather than masking symptoms with medication.

Learn more

Stroke Recovery

Enhancing neuroplasticity after stroke to support motor, speech, and cognitive rehabilitation.

Post-stroke rehabilitation benefits significantly from neuroplasticity enhancement. TMS and TPS can be used alongside physiotherapy to stimulate underactive brain regions, accelerate neural reorganisation, and improve functional recovery in motor, speech, and cognitive domains.

Learn more

Neurological Rehabilitation

Supporting recovery and function in conditions including Parkinson's, TBI, and motor disorders.

Neurological conditions affecting movement, cognition, and daily function may benefit from carefully targeted neuromodulation. ANAVA's protocols are built around individual assessments, combining brain stimulation with evidence-based rehabilitation to support meaningful functional gains.

Learn more

Anxiety Disorders

For anxiety that persists despite medication and therapy, targeted neuromodulation offers a new dimension of treatment.

Anxiety involves dysregulation in specific neural circuits — particularly those governing threat response and emotional processing. Neurotherapy can modulate these networks, reducing the overactivation that underlies chronic anxiety, often as an adjunct to existing psychological care.

Learn more

PTSD & Trauma

Where trauma continues to hold patterns in the body and brain, neurotherapy can support integration and recovery.

PTSD involves persistent neurological changes in memory consolidation, emotional regulation, and threat processing. Neuromodulation approaches, used alongside trauma-informed psychological support, may help recalibrate these patterns and reduce the burden of intrusive symptoms.

Learn more

Our therapies

Evidence-based neurotherapy,
delivered with precision

ANAVA offers three core brain stimulation modalities, selected and combined based on each patient's individual profile and clinical needs.

tDCS
Brain Stimulation

Transcranial Direct Current Stimulation

tDCS

A gentle, low-level electrical current modulates neuronal excitability in targeted brain regions. Safe, silent, and typically experienced as a mild tingling sensation.

  • Depression & mood disorders
  • Chronic pain modulation
  • Cognitive enhancement in rehabilitation
Learn more about tDCS
TMS
Gold Standard

Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation

TMS

Magnetic pulses stimulate specific cortical regions with established, peer-reviewed protocols. TMS has regulatory approval for depression in multiple countries and carries an exceptional safety profile.

  • FDA-cleared for treatment-resistant depression
  • Anxiety and OCD protocols
  • Neurological rehabilitation support
Learn more about TMS
TPS
Emerging Therapy

Transcranial Pulse Stimulation

TPS

An advanced ultrasound-based therapy capable of reaching deep brain structures. Particularly promising in neurological rehabilitation and Alzheimer's research, TPS represents the frontier of non-invasive neuromodulation.

  • Deep neurological structures
  • Neurological rehabilitation
  • Cognitive decline support
Learn more about TPS
